Born in the Anthropocene

An interactive narrative exploring a sustainable future based in collective welfare and critical thought

This is a design futuring project assigned to demonstrate competencies in p5.js and with key concepts of computational media and design. We collaborated to employ these skillsets and design a sustainable future aligned with one or more UN Sustainable Development Goals, using fabulation as our main approach.

Goals

Propose a sustainable future society. Develop a p5.js sketch or other interactive program to show this society

Results

An interactive narrative and integrate p5.js sketch where users make social/cultural decisions in the proposed future that determine the sustainability score used to determine the kind of city they can build

Tools

  • Twine
  • p5.js

Problem/Research Question

Before we can work toward more positive futures, we have to imagine them. Work in a team to imagine a more sustainable future society.

Despite the promising and novel innovations enabled by the advent of artificial intelligence (AI), this development has also had immediate and severe environmental impacts predicted to continue. In the face of rapidly changing climate and this new, ubiquitous environmental pressor, we must find and develop more sustainable structure for future generations.

Background and Theoretical Framework

UN Sustainable Development Goals

The UN Sustainable Development Goals incorporated into this project were No Poverty (1), Zero Hunger (2), Quality Education (4), and Sustainable Cities and Communities (11). We decided to focus on the social/cultural elements of sustainability to demonstrate how community, cooperation, skepticism, and education all play pivotal roles in our collective well-being and understanding of sustainability.

Findings/Insights

Reflection

This project received high marks from our instructional team for the course, Introduction to Computational Media, meaning we successfully proposed a sustainable future and designed an interactive artifact that allowed for the exploration of said future. We were also applauded for expanding beyond the constraints of p5.js and incorporating other tools.

This project was a valuable opportunity to improve my understanding of Twine as a prototyping/development tool for hypertext narratives. I successfully translated a traditional narrative into hypertext, considering the branches of the narrative and how different choices allowed for the exploration of key themes in our proposed future.

As my first foray into design futuring, it was fascinating to see how I may use interactive narratives as sites for speculative fiction. With more time, I would've liked to better establish the world of our proposed future, creating a cohesive and solid background upon which to explore the social and cultural determinants of sustainability.
